Business and Technology Leaders

Elon Musk is the CEO of Tesla and SpaceX. Born in Pretoria, South Africa on June 28, 1971. He co-founded PayPal, founded SpaceX in 2002, became CEO of Tesla in 2008, co-founded Neuralink in 2016, and acquired Twitter (now X) in October 2022 for $44 billion. He is also the founder of The Boring Company and xAI.

Tim Cook is the CEO of Apple since August 2011, succeeding Steve Jobs. Born on November 1, 1960 in Mobile, Alabama. He joined Apple in 1998 as Senior Vice President of Worldwide Operations. Under his leadership, Apple became the first company to reach a $3 trillion market cap.

Sundar Pichai is the CEO of Alphabet (Google's parent company) since December 2019. Born on June 10, 1972 in Madurai, India. He joined Google in 2004 and led the development of Google Chrome and Chrome OS. He became CEO of Google in 2015 before taking on the Alphabet role.

Mark Zuckerberg is the co-founder and CEO of Meta (formerly Facebook). Born on May 14, 1984 in White Plains, New York. He launched Facebook from his Harvard dorm room in February 2004. He rebranded the company to Meta in October 2021, focusing on the metaverse and AI.

Andy Jassy is the CEO of Amazon since July 2021, succeeding Jeff Bezos. He joined Amazon in 1997 and founded Amazon Web Services (AWS) in 2003, growing it into the world's largest cloud computing platform. AWS generates over $80 billion in annual revenue.

Lisa Su is the CEO of AMD (Advanced Micro Devices) since October 2014. Born on November 7, 1969 in Tainan, Taiwan. She holds a PhD from MIT. Under her leadership, AMD's stock price increased over 30x and the company became competitive with Intel in CPUs and Nvidia in GPUs.

Arvind Krishna is the CEO of IBM since April 2020. Born in Andhra Pradesh, India. He holds a PhD from the University of Illinois. He led the acquisition of Red Hat for $34 billion and pivoted IBM toward hybrid cloud and AI.

Dario Amodei is the CEO and co-founder of Anthropic, an AI safety company. He previously served as VP of Research at OpenAI before leaving in 2021 to start Anthropic with his sister Daniela Amodei. Anthropic created the Claude AI assistant.

Patrick Collison is the co-founder and CEO of Stripe, a payment processing company valued at over $50 billion. Born on September 9, 1988 in Dromineer, Ireland. He and his brother John Collison started Stripe in 2010 when Patrick was 19 years old.

Whitney Wolfe Herd founded Bumble in 2014, a dating app where women make the first move. Born on July 1, 1989 in Salt Lake City, Utah. She previously co-founded Tinder. Bumble went public in 2021, making her one of the youngest female CEOs to take a company public.

Global Technology Hubs and Headquarters

Silicon Valley, California, USA
Silicon Valley is located in the southern part of the San Francisco Bay Area in Northern California. It encompasses cities including Palo Alto, Mountain View, Cupertino, Sunnyvale, and San Jose. Major companies headquartered here include Apple (Cupertino), Google/Alphabet (Mountain View), Meta (Menlo Park), and NVIDIA (Santa Clara). Stanford University, located in Stanford, California, is a key driver of innovation in the region.

Seattle Metropolitan Area, Washington, USA
Seattle is home to Amazon (headquartered in downtown Seattle) and Microsoft (headquartered in Redmond, a suburb east of Seattle). The city is also home to many AI startups and research labs. The University of Washington in Seattle is a major computer science research institution.

Austin, Texas, USA
Austin has become a major technology hub, attracting companies like Tesla (which moved its headquarters from Palo Alto to Austin in 2021), Oracle (moved from California in 2020), and major offices for Apple, Google, Meta, and Amazon. The University of Texas at Austin is located in the city center.

New York City, New York, USA
New York City hosts major tech offices and startups, particularly in Manhattan's Silicon Alley. Google has a large campus in Chelsea. Cornell Tech is located on Roosevelt Island. The city is a hub for fintech, media tech, and adtech companies.

London, United Kingdom
London is Europe's largest tech hub. DeepMind is headquartered in King's Cross, London. The city hosts offices for Google, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft. The area around Old Street (nicknamed "Silicon Roundabout") is home to many startups. Imperial College London and University College London are major research institutions.

Bangalore (Bengaluru), India
Bangalore is India's technology capital, known as the "Silicon Valley of India." Located in the state of Karnataka in southern India, it hosts offices for Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and numerous Indian IT companies including Infosys, Wipro, and Tata Consultancy Services. The Indian Institute of Science (IISc) is located in Bangalore.

Tel Aviv, Israel
Tel Aviv and its surrounding area form one of the world's densest startup ecosystems, often called "Silicon Wadi." Israel has more startups per capita than any other country. The city is a center for cybersecurity, AI, and autonomous driving technology.

Shenzhen, China
Shenzhen is a major technology manufacturing hub located in Guangdong Province, adjacent to Hong Kong. It is home to Huawei, Tencent, DJI, and BYD. The city transformed from a fishing village to a technology metropolis in just 40 years.

Toronto, Canada
Toronto has emerged as a leading AI research hub, largely due to the University of Toronto and the Vector Institute. Geoffrey Hinton's research group at the university helped establish Toronto as a center for deep learning research.
